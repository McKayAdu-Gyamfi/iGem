The Story

Once upon a time, on a beautiful beach in a small village by the sea, a
bright orange fish named Tetra lay stretched on the shore. He wiggled and
squirmed, but he was stuck! A plastic net had tangled him up, and no
matter how hard he tried, he couldn't break free. The waves gently
brushed against him, but Tetra couldn't swim back into the ocean. He
needed help, and fast.

TETRA, THE STORY

Just as Tetra's hope was feeling, a boy named Jules
and his grandpa Henri came walking along the
beach. Julles spotted Tetra right away. "Look,
Grandpa!" he shouted, running over to the little
fish. "We have to help him!" With careful hands,
Jules worked to untangle the net wrapped around
Tetra, while Grandpa Henri watched with a smile.
The fish's eyes sparkled with gratitude. Finally,
with one last pull, Jules freed Tetra!

The bright orange fish flapped happily, and with a
flick of his tail, he splashed back into the ocean.

"Goodbye, little friend!" Jules called, smiling as
Tetra disappeared into the water.

From that moment on, Tetra, Jules, and Henri
shared a special bond. They came to the beach
every day, watching Tetra swim and play, laughing
as he leaped from the waves.

But then...

Inspired by Tetra and the memories of Jules, Henri
knew this was his chance to make a difference. He
contacted Ashesi iGem team, eager to join their
project. Together, they worked hard to collect plastic
waste and turn it into strong, pentacones. Henri
poured his heart into the project, knowing that this
was a way to honor Jules and help heal the ocean.

FINAL MESSAGE FROM TETRA:

"Tetra, Henri, and Jules's story shows that we are all connected--to each other
and the ocean. When we protect our oceans, we protect life itself. Join Tetra
and the Ashesi iGem team to reduce plastic pollution and help our oceans thrive.
Together, we can make a difference and create a brighter future.

To be continued..."

OUR PROJECT FOCUS

Our project goal is to create a novel solution that combines degradation of PET plastics and protecting
coastal communities from coastal erosion. This will build ecosystems that are resilient to climate change and
human induced plastic pollution in the sea.
